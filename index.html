Fyodor Dostoevsky

Summary

Seminal Russian novelist, philosopher, and essayist whose work profoundly shaped modern literature, psychology, and existential philosophy. Renowned for deep explorations of moral responsibility, faith, suffering, free will, and the psychology of guilt. Widely regarded as one of the most influential writers of the 19th century, with enduring global impact across literature, theology, philosophy, and political thought.
